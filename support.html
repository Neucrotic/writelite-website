Why Support WriteLite

Hello, I'm NEL — a writer who builds things.

Long before I wrote a line of code, I wrote stories. Primarily fiction, I have also enjoyed writing poetry, essays and I maintain a daily journalling habit. Over the years, nearly every writing tool I reached for felt like it got in the way — bloated with features I'd never touch, asking me to wrangle a project before I'd written a sentence.

I wanted something simple. Software that opens fast, gets out of the way, and lets the writer write. Free to use, but genuinely capable — a robust set of tools for serious writing, with none of the clutter that breaks your focus. I looked for it for a long time. When I couldn't find it, I decided to build it myself.

That's WriteLite. The writing app I always wanted and never found.

For a while I built it in the margins — evenings, days off, whatever hours I could carve out around work. Then, over the long weekend June, I lost my job without notice. It's easy for bumps in the road like this to push back the projects we maintain on the side. They can delay us weeks, or even months. It would have been easy to call this another setback.

Instead, I've decided to treat it as a beginning. With enough savings to get me through the month, a working protoype undergoing polish and and a clear roadmap for a robust set of features, I'm pouring the time I suddenly have into WriteLite — pushing development harder than I ever could when I was maintaining a full-time job. I'm building the thing I believe should exist, properly and out in the open.

That's where you come in.

WriteLite is free. It stands a testiment to my ethos that writing tools shouldn't hide behind a pay-wall, be restrained to an operating system or hide the words we write in files we can't access, but building it full-time while out of work is its own kind of tightrope. If WriteLite is something you'd want to exist — or something you already use and value — a small contribution goes further than you'd think. Enough to pay a bill. Enough to fill a plate. Enough to keep the lights on and the ball rolling while I build.

Nothing here is expected. Every bit is appreciated. Even the simple act of sharing this with another writer helps keep this dream moving forward.

For that I am eternally grateful.

— NEL
